Scientific Evidence on Egg Benefits

Below are selected studies highlighting the nutritional and health advantages of consuming eggs as part of a balanced diet.

Eggs Are Nutrient-Dense and Rich in Choline, Protein, and Antioxidants

Eggs provide high-quality protein, all essential amino acids, key vitamins (A, B12, D, E), minerals (selenium, phosphorus), choline, lutein and zeaxanthin—nutrients crucial for eye health and overall vitality.

Healthline, 2024

Egg Intake Not Linked to Heart Disease; May Reduce Coronary Risk

A meta-analysis of 23 prospective studies (1.4M+ participants) found that consuming more than 1 egg per day was not associated with higher cardiovascular disease risk and was linked to lower risk of coronary artery disease.

Zhong et al., 2020

Moderate Egg Consumption Linked to Lower CVD and Overall Mortality in Older Adults

In a large cohort of adults aged 70+, those eating 1–6 eggs per week had a 29 % lower risk of death from cardiovascular disease and 17 % lower all-cause mortality, compared to those who rarely ate eggs.

Monash University cohort, Nutrients, 2025

Two Eggs Daily Improve Vascular Function and Gut Microbiota without Inflammation

In healthy young subjects, daily intake of two hard‑boiled eggs enhanced endothelial function (FMD, baPWV) and positively affected gut microbiota, without raising markers of inflammation, lipids or oxidative stress.

Randomized study, 2022
