Effects of Prolonged Mask Use: Scientific Evidence

Effects of Surgical and Cloth Masks on Vital Signs

This study examined the effects of surgical and cloth masks on heart rate, oxygen saturation, and transcutaneous carbon dioxide levels both at rest and during physical activity. Results showed no significant impact on these parameters.

B. Fikenzer et al., PLOS ONE, 2020

Impact of N95 Mask Use on CO₂ and Oxygen Levels

This research evaluated the impact of wearing N95 masks on carbon dioxide levels, heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation in healthy individuals. Findings indicated no significant changes in these physiological parameters.

S. Swaminathan et al., ScienceDirect, 2024

Systematic Review on Mask Use and Gas Exchange

A systematic review compiling various studies on surgical and FFP2/KN95 mask use, analyzing their effects on gas exchange, lung function, and physical performance. The review confirmed no significant negative impact on these parameters.

M. Hopkins et al., Sports Medicine - Open, 2021

Effects of Masks on CO₂ and Vital Signs in Pilots

This study investigated the effects of cloth and paper masks on carbon dioxide levels, heart rate, respiratory rate, and oxygen saturation in pilot instructors, finding no significant alterations.

J. Kim et al., Aerosol and Air Quality Research, 2020

Mask Use in Individuals with Preexisting Respiratory Conditions

Some studies suggest minimal effects of prolonged mask use in individuals with chronic respiratory diseases like COPD; however, these effects are generally minor and do not indicate significant risk of hypoxia or acidosis.

FAA Medical Report, 2024
