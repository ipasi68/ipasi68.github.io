Whole Fruit, Smoothies, and Fructose: What Science Says

Whole Fruit Fiber Reduces Fructose Absorption

This study shows that the fiber in whole fruit significantly slows down the absorption of fructose and reduces the glycemic and insulin response, unlike fruit juice or pure fructose.

Gaby et al., Journal of Nutritional Biochemistry, 2012

Blending Fruit May Spike Glycemia

Researchers found that blending fruit disrupts fiber structure, potentially raising postprandial blood sugar more than whole fruit, even if all nutrients are retained.

Liu et al., Nutrition and Diabetes, 2019

Isolated Fructose and Liver Fat Accumulation

High intake of isolated fructose (e.g. in syrups or added sugars) is associated with increased liver fat and insulin resistance, unlike fructose consumed within whole fruit.

Stanhope et al., Journal of Clinical Investigation, 2009
