Studies on the Benefits of Acetic Acid

Blood Sugar Control

Taking acetic acid before meals can reduce blood sugar spikes and improve insulin sensitivity in people with type 2 diabetes.

Valdes et al., 2021 – Meta-analysis on acetic acid and blood sugar

Effects on Body Weight

Acetic acid may help reduce visceral fat accumulation and improve feelings of fullness, contributing to slight weight loss.

Kondo et al., 2009 – Study on fat reduction

Antimicrobial Activity

Acetic acid is effective against bacteria such as E. coli and Salmonella, useful for food preservation and hygiene.

Pubtexto, 2024 – Review on antimicrobial properties

Cardiovascular Benefits

Some animal studies suggest it may lower blood pressure and improve lipid profiles.

Fushimi et al., 2001 – Blood pressure reduction in hypertensive rats

Side Effects and Precautions

Acetic acid can cause unwanted effects such as tooth enamel erosion, esophageal irritation, and interference with hypoglycemic medications, especially if taken undiluted or in excessive amounts.

Kondo et al., 2009 – Observations on gastrointestinal side effects
